The merchant problem

Merchants do not just need more data. They need to know what deserves attention today.

“Why am I not getting sales?”

Early merchants often have many possible fixes: product page, pricing, traffic source, trust signals, shipping, discounts, or offer quality.

“Is this campaign working?”

The merchant job is not to admire engagement metrics. It is to understand which channel is actually creating profitable customer demand.

“What should I fix first?”

The hardest part is sequencing. A good product does not only surface signals; it helps merchants pick the next useful action.

The critique

Proactive AI is only useful if merchants trust it.

Generic recommendation

“Sales are down this week. Try improving your product pages.”

This creates more work for the merchant. It does not explain why the signal matters, what evidence supports it, or what safe action comes next.

Decision-grade recommendation

“Mobile conversion is down 14% vs. baseline, mainly on high-traffic PDPs. Test simplified variant selection through Rollouts.”

This connects the signal to evidence, the evidence to a decision, and the decision to a low-risk next step.

Product principle

Every Pulse recommendation should answer five questions.

This is the difference between an AI notification feed and a merchant decision layer.

  1. Why now?
    What changed, and why is this surfacing today?
  2. What evidence?
    Which store signals, targets, or trends support the recommendation?
  3. What action?
    What is the specific next step the merchant can take?
  4. How confident?
    Is this a strong signal, a weak signal, or an exploration?
  5. How can I control it?
    Apply, dismiss, snooze, investigate, or test safely.
